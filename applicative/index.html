Applicative

Applicative types are Apply types with one extra function, which we define in Fantasy Land as “of“: With “of”, we can take a value, and lift it into the given Applicative. Thatâ€™s it! In the wild, most Apply types you practically use will also be Applicative.

With an applicative, our values are wrapped in a context, just like Functors:

But our functions are wrapped in a context too!

Applicative functor must know, how to apply a function wrapped in a context to a value wrapped in a context:

Example (Haskell):

> Just (+3) <*> Just 2
-- Just 5
> [(*2), (+3)] <*> [1, 2, 3]
-- [2, 4, 6, 4, 5, 6]
> (*) <$> Just 5 <*> Just 3
-- Just 15
